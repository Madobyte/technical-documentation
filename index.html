What's new in iOS 13

Dark Mode A new Dark Mode option gives the entire iOS experience a beautiful dark color scheme that’s perfect for low-light environments. Quickly turn on Dark Mode from Control Center, or set it to automatically turn on at night. When Dark Mode is turned on, the light from your device won’t disturb the people around you.

Photos Browse your photo library by days, months, and years so it’s easy to find and share your photos and videos. As you scroll, Live Photos and videos play, bringing your photo library to life. Redesigned editing tools make it easy for you to apply changes to your photos and see at a glance where those changes were made.

Camera Portrait Lighting now lets you virtually adjust the intensity of each studio lighting effect. On supported models, a new Portrait Lighting effect, High-Key Light Mono, creates a beautiful look with a grayscale subject on a white background.

Maps Rebuilt from the ground up, Maps features more realistic details for roads, beaches, parks, and buildings. Explore where you’re going before you get there with Look Around, build collections of your favorite locations, share your ETA, and more.

Sign in with Apple Sign in to apps and websites quickly and easily using the Apple ID you already have. No filling out forms or creating new passwords — just tap the Sign in with Apple button in any participating app or on a website, use Face ID or Touch ID to complete sign in, and you’re all set. Apple doesn’t track your profile and protects your Apple ID with two-factor authentication.

Siri The new Siri voice sounds incredibly natural, particularly when speaking longer phrases. Siri also offers personalized suggestions when you search in Podcasts, Safari, and Maps, and can detect reminders in Messages.

Animoji and Memoji Three new Animoji characters—mouse, octopus, and cow—give you more options to express yourself. Make your own Memoji one in a trillion with new hairstyles, headwear, makeup, and piercings. Automatically generate sticker packs based on your Animoji and Memoji characters that allow you to express a range of emotions in Messages.

Messages Automatically share your name and photo when you start a conversation. You can use an Animoji, an image, or a monogram for your photo.

Announce Messages (iOS 13.2) When you wear AirPods Pro, AirPods (2nd generation), or other supported headphones, Siri can read your incoming messages, and you can speak a reply for Siri to send.

Set communication limits (iOS 13.3) Use Screen Time to limit who your children can communicate with—and who can communicate with them—throughout the day and during downtime.

App Store Subscribe to Apple Arcade (not available in all countries or regions), a service that gives you access to new games on iPhone, iPad, iPod touch, Mac, and Apple TV. With a single subscription, you can download and play any Apple Arcade game from the App Store, without ads or additional purchases.

Apple TV+ Subscribe and watch new and exclusive Apple Originals, ad-free and on demand. Watch Apple TV+ (not available in all countries or regions) in the Apple TV app on your iPhone, iPad, iPod touch, Mac, Apple TV, and supported smart TVs and streaming devices.

Fonts You can now get fonts from the App Store to use in documents you create on iPhone, iPad, and iPod touch.

Keyboard Use QuickPath (not available for all languages) to enter a word by sliding your finger from one letter to the next. The keyboard recognizes the path you draw and converts it for you, making one‑handed typing a breeze.

Audio Sharing Share what you’re listening to with a friend who’s also wearing AirPods or compatible Beats headphones or earphones.

Health A more personalized Health app makes it easier than ever to access the information that matters most to you. With the new Cycle Tracking option, you can keep track of your menstrual cycle, set up fertility notifications, and more.

Emergency access to your Medical ID (iOS 13.5) You can allow your Medical ID information to be sent automatically to emergency services when you call or text 911 or use Emergency SOS from your iPhone or Apple Watch (U.S. only).

Reminders The all-new Reminders app makes it easier than ever to create and organize reminders. Use the quick toolbar to add dates, times, locations, flags, photos, and even scanned documents to your reminders.

Shortcuts The Shortcuts app is now built into iOS 13 and is the home for all your shortcuts. Get started with Shortcuts by adding a personalized daily routine to help streamline your day.

HomeKit Secure Video (iOS 13.2) Footage from your home security cameras is end-to-end encrypted and stored securely in iCloud. Only you and the people you share the footage with can view it.

CarPlay An all-new CarPlay dashboard gives you a single place to keep track of maps, audio controls, and Siri suggestions. Easily view the Calendar app to take a glance at the day ahead, then tap for directions or to dial in to a meeting.

Performance Face ID unlocks your device up to 30 percent faster in iOS 13. Apps open up to two times faster, and apps from the App Store are packaged in a new way that makes them up to 50 percent smaller.

Privacy and security Control the location data that you share with apps. You can choose to grant an app access to your location once or anytime you use it. When you receive alerts about an app that’s using your location in the background, you can decide whether to update your permissions.

Battery information A new option helps slow the rate of battery aging by reducing the time your iPhone spends fully charged.

Learn trick for iOS 13 The Tips app adds new suggestions frequently, so you can get the most from your iPhone

Note: New features and apps may vary depending on your iPhone model, region, language, and carrier.

Next-gen Features

Say hello to the future

Get to know iPhone 11, iPhone 11 Pro, and iPhone 11 Pro Max. Learn intuitive gestures, use your face as your password, and discover what’s possible with the amazing Ultra Wide camera.

It’s all screen

The device is the display—an edge-to-edge screen you can quickly navigate with familiar gestures. Swipe up from the bottom edge to return to the Home screen, swipe up and pause to see your open apps, or swipe down from the top right to reveal Control Center.

Unlock with a glance

Use Face ID to unlock your iPhone, authenticate purchases, and use Apple Pay. Face ID is a powerful and secure authentication system that’s even more convenient than Touch ID. To set up Face ID, go to Settings > Face ID & Passcode.

Get the big picture

The Ultra Wide camera on iPhone 11, iPhone 11 Pro, and iPhone 11 Pro Max captures area outside the camera frame that you can include when you take a photo, or add later when you edit in Photos.

Camera Features

One camera for all the shots

Use your iPhone to take great shots in any situation. From a candid photo to a studio-quality portrait—you can take them all with your iPhone camera.

Never miss a moment

With your iPhone nearby, you’re always ready to capture a great photo. To quickly open Camera, swipe left on the Lock screen. Camera automatically focuses the shot and adjusts the exposure. If you want to zoom in on your subject, touch and hold 1x (on supported models), then drag the slider to the left. Tap the Shutter button to take the shot.

Shoot portraits like a pro

On supported models, use Portrait mode to create stunning portraits that use a depth-of-field effect—your subject stays sharp while the background blurs. Switch to Portrait mode, then drag the slider to choose a lighting effect. On iPhone XS and later, tap the Depth Adjustment button to adjust the background blur, or the Lighting Adjustment button to fine-tune the intensity of the studio lighting effects.

Record video in Photo mode

On iPhone SE (2nd generation), iPhone 11, iPhone 11 Pro, and iPhone 11 Pro Max, you can record a QuickTake video in Photo mode. Touch and hold the Shutter button to start recording a QuickTake video. Slide the Shutter button to the right, then let go over the white circle to continue recording hands-free.

While you record a QuickTake video, you can tap the Shutter button to take a still photo at the same time.

Capture more in your shot

Use the Ultra Wide camera on iPhone 11, iPhone 11 Pro, and iPhone 11 Pro Max to increase the camera’s field of view and capture even more in the frame. To zoom out in Photo or Video mode, touch and hold 1x, then drag the slider to the right. To fit more in a selfie, tap the Front Facing camera button, then tap the Zoom Out button to enlarge the frame and include more of what’s around you.

Travel with your iPhone

Change the way you travel

iPhone makes traveling a breeze—use Maps to scout locations before your trip, and make collections of must-see attractions. Before you leave for the airport, check your flight status and terminal location. While you’re traveling, keep credit cards and travel passes organized and secure in Wallet. When you get home, use Photos to view and share your memories with friends.

Organize must-see places

Create a collection in Maps to keep track of the places you want to visit on your trip. To get started, find a place in Maps you want to visit, swipe up, then tap “Add to.” Tap New Collection, then give the collection a name. Continue adding places to the collection, or make new collections for different activities, such as restaurants you want to try, local attractions you want to visit, or neighborhoods you want to explore. When a collection is complete, tap the Share button to share it with your fellow travelers.

Check your flight status

Reduce stress by checking the status of your flight before you leave for the airport. You can confirm your departure time, get the gate location, and check other flight information. Swipe down from the middle of the Home screen, enter your flight number in the search field, then select your flight in the search results.

Keep cards and passes in one place

Use Wallet to keep track of credit cards, boarding passes, tickets, and transit cards while you travel. Look for the Add to Apple Wallet option on websites, in emails, or in text messages. Some passes in Wallet appear automatically based on the time or your location. For example, when you arrive at the airport, boarding passes for your flight appear on your iPhone screen.

Relive the memories

Use Photos to find pictures and videos from a trip. Tap Photos, then tap Months to see your photos organized by trips and significant events. Tap the More Options button to play a movie of the trip or to share photos and videos with friends and family. You can also quickly search for photos— just tap Search, type something in the search field like “California 2016” or “camping,” then select a result.

Health and Wellness Features

Track your health and wellness goals with iPhone

iPhone can help you track data about your health and reach your wellness goals. Use the Health app to monitor your activity levels and track your menstrual cycle. Find apps in the App Store to support mindfulness or meditation, and use the Bedtime feature in Clock to maintain a regular bedtime routine.

Movement matters

Carry iPhone with you to count your steps and how many flights of stairs you climb each day. Open the Health app to check your daily activity so far. For more details, tap Flights Climbed or Steps, then choose the weekly, monthly, or yearly view to see your activity progress over time.

Calm your mind

Taking a few deep breaths and quieting your mind is a great way to relieve stress. Use the Breathe app on Apple Watch or download a meditation app from the App Store. Tap Today to browse featured apps in the App Store, or tap Search, then type “meditation” or “mindfulness” in the search field. No matter which app you choose, iPhone records your mindful minutes each day in the Health app.

Track your cycle

Gain insight into your menstrual cycle by tracking symptoms, intensity, and duration. Find out when your period is likely to begin, get a notification when your fertile window is approaching, and view your cycle history. To get started, open the Health app, tap Browse, then tap Cycle Tracking.

Start a bedtime routine

Use Bedtime in the Clock app to help restore your body by consistently going to bed and waking up at the same time every day. When Bedtime is turned on, your display dims and notifications are silenced until you unlock your iPhone in the morning. Open Clock, tap Bedtime, then follow the onscreen instructions.
